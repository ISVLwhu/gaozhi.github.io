Biography

  • Recipient of the National Young Thousand Talents Program
  • Distinguished Professor of the “Chutian Scholars Program”, Hubei Province
  • Hubei Provincial Outstanding Young Scientist
  • Principal Investigator of Major Projects funded by the National Natural Science Foundation of China (NSFC)
  • Principal Investigator of the Innovation Research Group Program, Hubei Provincial Natural Science Foundation
  • Principal Investigator of China–ASEAN International Cooperation Projects
  • Founding Expert, United Nations Global Big Data Center

Prof. Gao has long been engaged in fundamental and frontier research on artificial intelligence, real-time remote sensing, and intelligent unmanned systems. He previously held academic and research positions at the National University of Singapore, the University of Illinois at Urbana–Champaign, and the University of Oxford.

He has published over 100 papers in leading international journals and top-tier conferences, including IEEE Transactions on Pattern Analysis and Machine Intelligence (TPAMI), International Journal of Computer Vision (IJCV), ISPRS Journal of Photogrammetry and Remote Sensing (ISPRS JPRS), and CVPR. His research achievements have been recognized with more than ten major scientific and technological awards, including the Second Prize of the Military Science and Technology Progress Award, the Second Prize of the Invention and Entrepreneurship Innovation Award of the China Invention Association, the First Prize of the Guangdong Emergency and Safety Science and Technology Progress Award, the First Prize of the China Geomatics Science and Technology Progress Award, and the Silver Medal at the Geneva International Exhibition of Inventions.

Prof. Gao proposed a brain-inspired cognitive domain-specific large model for anomaly recognition, leading his team to win the Global Championship of the CVPR Visual Anomaly Detection Challenge. He also developed an end-to-end flight control technology, which won dual championships (indoor and outdoor categories) at the IMAV World Drone Competition held in Toulouse, France. His work has received more than ten Best Paper and Outstanding Paper Awards at major international and domestic conferences.
